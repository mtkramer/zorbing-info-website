Pure Zorbing and It's Evil Twin

Zorbing is a popular activity in many countries outside of the United States. It typically involves traveling by running or walking the zorb around a course. For a little more excitement, riders are strapped in and the zorb is rolled down a hill. Zorbs can be used in all weather condidtions including in the snow and on the water.

Zorbing should not be confused with water balling which is not only much less safe that zorbing, but illegal in some states.

Zorb Construction

Zorbs are most commonly composed of two layers of durable pvc with an air cusion between them to soften external impacts with objects and to soften internal impacts from occupants bouncing against walls. Water balls only have one layer to minimally allow them to float with a rider walking or crawling inside. Zorbers have the option to run or walk or to be imobilized by being strapped down for high speed rolling. The last important difference is that Zorbs are dimpled to anchor the outside and inside layers to each other which strengthens the cusioning effects and zorb durability.
